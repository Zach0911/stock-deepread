# Evidence Grading

Use evidence grades in the source register and in key findings.

| Grade | Sources | Can support |
|---|---|---|
| A | Regulator filings, exchange announcements, company IR originals, official structured data | Core facts and financial numbers |
| B | Earnings-call transcript/webcast, results presentation, management interview, official roadshow | Management tone and claims, cross-checked with A-grade facts |
| C | Reputable industry data, mainstream financial media, public analyst summaries, peer filings | Context, external validation, expectations |
| D | Social media, forums, KOLs, X, Reddit, YouTube comments, Stocktwits, Snowball, Eastmoney Guba, Futu comments, Xiaohongshu | Market narrative, attention, sentiment clues only |
| E | AI inference, estimates, model interpolation, missing-data fill | Explicitly marked inference only |

## Hard Boundaries

- A core conclusion needs A/B/C support or a visible "inference" label.
- D/E sources cannot independently support factual claims, valuation claims, or buy/sell conclusions.
- If sources conflict, state the conflict and lower confidence.
- If a full analyst report or transcript is unavailable, do not pretend to have read it.
- Do not quote long passages from copyrighted reports/transcripts. Summarize and cite links.

## Research Notes

When the user asks to interpret a research report and no full text is provided:

1. Search public rating changes, target-price changes, media summaries, and company disclosures.
2. State whether the full report was obtained.
3. Reconstruct only the publicly visible thesis.
4. Compare assumptions against company filings and calls.
5. Mark inaccessible or paywalled details as unavailable.

## Social Media Boundary

Social media can answer:

- What narrative is spreading?
- Is attention rising?
- Which claim should be verified?
- Could positioning or hype explain price action?

Social media cannot prove:

- revenue, orders, margins, customer wins, supply agreements
- management intent
- analyst forecasts
- valuation fair value
- a buy/sell/hold action
