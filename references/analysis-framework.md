# Analysis Framework

Use this before writing conclusions. The goal is a deep read, not a summary.

## Disclosure Deepread Loop

1. Conclusion first: what changed, why it matters, what could disprove it.
2. Fact base: business model, segments, geography, customers, margins, cash flow, debt, capex, valuation, recent market move.
3. Change detection: YoY, QoQ, guidance, consensus, previous call language, previous filing language.
4. Business machine: identify the drivers that actually move revenue, margin, cash flow, and valuation.
5. Supply chain: map at least 3 layers from end demand to upstream constraints.
6. Financial translation: convert the story into revenue, gross margin, operating margin, cash flow, working capital, capex, dilution, or valuation.
7. Management language: distinguish prepared remarks, Q&A, direct answers, vague answers, missing metrics.
8. External views: compare public analyst/news claims against primary disclosures.
9. Disconfirming evidence: write the bear case before final recommendations.

## Business-Machine Guide

| Business type | Watch |
|---|---|
| Manufacturing/auto | volume, ASP, utilization, raw materials, inventory, orders, capex |
| Internet/platform | GMV, take rate, MAU/DAU, merchants, ad load, fulfillment cost, subsidy |
| SaaS/software | ARR, NRR, churn, RPO, billings, SBC, sales efficiency |
| Financials | NIM, asset quality, provisions, capital ratio, duration, liquidity |
| Consumer | same-store sales, channel inventory, price tier, promotions, brand momentum |
| Healthcare/biotech | pipeline, clinical milestones, commercialization, patents, cash runway |
| Semiconductors/hardware | design wins, yield, node, packaging, capacity, customer qualification, inventory cycle |

## Supply-Chain and Chokepoint Test

Map:

```text
End demand -> System/platform -> Module/component -> Equipment/material/capacity -> Raw material/policy/energy
```

For each layer, ask:

- Is demand expanding structurally or temporarily?
- Is this layer hard to replace?
- Is expansion slow?
- Is customer validation confirmed, claimed, inferred, or unknown?
- Does the company have pricing power?
- Is the financial sensitivity large enough to matter?
- What would disprove the chokepoint thesis?

## Financial Translation

Every major claim should map to at least one metric:

- revenue: volume, price, mix, FX, acquisitions, one-offs
- gross margin: price, cost, utilization, mix, write-downs
- operating margin: opex discipline, sales leverage, R&D, SBC
- cash flow: profit quality, inventory, receivables, payables, capex
- balance sheet: cash, debt, convertibles, warrants, dilution, buybacks
- valuation: P/E, EV/S, EV/EBITDA, P/B, SOTP, DCF assumptions

## Management-Language Read

Separate:

- prepared remarks: what management wants investors to hear
- analyst Q&A: what the market is worried about
- directness: direct, partial, vague, avoided
- new language: newly emphasized terms
- missing language: previously important terms that disappeared
- consistency: whether claims match orders, margins, inventory, cash flow, and customer evidence
